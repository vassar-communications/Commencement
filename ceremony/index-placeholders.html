Your Speakers

Elizabeth Howe Bradley, President of Vassar College, Inaugurated September 2017

Before coming to Vassar, Elizabeth H. Bradley served as Director of the Brady-Johnson Program in Grand Strategy and Head of Branford College at Yale University. She was founder and faculty director of the Yale Global Health Leadership Institute, which operates education and research programs in six countries. During her tenure at Vassar, Bradley has established relationships with the University of Global Health Equity, a medical school in Rwanda, and with a secondary school for gifted students in Beijing. Last year, she forged a partnership with Columbia University’s Mailman School of Public Health that will enable Vassar students to earn a master’s degree in public health. Over the past two years she has worked with Vassar faculty, administrators, students, and staff to establish the Engaged Pluralism Initiative, which galvanizes new ways to make the campus more inclusive by enhancing a sense of belonging and community.

Welcoming Remarks Transcript

Jason Blum ’91, Commencement speaker, three-time Academy Award nominee, two-time Emmy Award-winning producer, and Vassar Trustee

A film major at Vassar, Jason Blum is founder and CEO of Blumhouse Productions, a multi-media company that pioneered a new model of studio filmmaking, producing high quality, microbudget films. He was nominated for Academy Awards for his work producing BlacKkKlansman, Get Out, and Whiplash. Blum has been recognized by TIME magazine’s 100 list of the world’s most influential people and has appeared several times on Vanity Fair’s New Establishment List. Blum said his Vassar experience had played a key role in shaping his future. “The courses I took at Vassar, as well as the other activities I was involved in, fueled my critical thinking skills and made me more curious about the world,” he said. “Those skills are imperative for anyone in the rapidly evolving entertainment industry.”
